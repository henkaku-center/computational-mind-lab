[Evidence for optimal semantic search throughout adulthood](/en/publications/#zemla2023),
led by Jeff Zemla with Diane Gooding, appeared in *Scientific Reports*.

Foraging models say a good searcher should exploit a patch until it thins out,
then move on. The finding here is that people do this near-optimally when
searching memory — and keep doing it across the adult lifespan, even as the
memory being searched changes.
