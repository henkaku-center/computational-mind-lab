Congratulations to **Zhuolun Zhong**, who led all three of the lab's papers at
the 48th Annual Meeting of the Cognitive Science Society — a remarkable showing.
Presentations and proceedings are available through the
[CogSci 2026 program](https://cognitivesciencesociety.org/program/#detail).

- [How the Teaching Style and Interpretation Type of State Interventions Shape
  Multi-Agent Coordination](/papers/files/Zhongetal2026CogScia.pdf) — with Luc
  Caspar.
- [Individual Differences in Human Teaching of Reinforcement Learning Agents:
  Evidence from Bayesian Hypothesis Testing](/papers/files/Zhongetal2026CogScib.pdf)
  — with Luc Caspar.
- [Interpretational alignment: How agents learn from physical guidance depends on
  how they interpret it](/papers/files/Zhongetal2026CogScic.pdf) — with Ben
  Prystawski, Sarah A. Wu, Bella Fascendini, and Sepehr Saeedpour. This one grew
  out of the "minimal paradigm" developed with students at the
  [COSMOS summer school](/en/news/2025-10-02-cosmos/).

See the [teaching by state intervention project](/en/projects/state-intervention/)
for how these fit together.
